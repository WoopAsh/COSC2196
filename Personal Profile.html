Learning Test

Tactile/Kinesthetic

“Tactile/Kinesthetic learners learn best when they can use their sense of touch and can move about. If you are a tactile/kinesthetic learner, the suggestions that follow can help you succeed in school to the best of your ability”.

• “Be physically active while you study. For example, rather than sit at your desk, occasionally walk back and forth with your textbook or notes as you read the information out loud.”

• “To decrease your fidgeting as you study, listen to music, preferably baroque music. However, discontinue this if you find the music to be distracting.”

• “Make extensive use of a computer and the Internet. Actively touching the keyboard will keep your mind active.”

What do the results of these tests mean for you?

I find it interesting, but I can’t entirely agree with all the personality traits. For example, I wouldn’t break and dance or sing. I can’t sing, but I like seeing everyone having a great time and will try and make sure they do. Also, I am a Tactile/Kinesthetic learner, loves to touch things. I didn’t particularly appreciate the Big 5 test and saying people with these traits don’t have creative skill.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team?

I will do well in a team environment. I love working in a team, and you can see from the personality test I work well with people. If you look at the Big 5 test, it is saying I’m a black and white person if that the case could cause tension in the group, not able to see the other person of view

How should you take this into account when forming a team?

Making sure I’m listening to people and hearing their views.

It helps me understand how people learn, and they could behave. However, I believe It is only a guideline of how a person will act; not all points are valid only some.
